When she was only 7 years old, Grace took apart her alarm clock to see how it worked. But she did not know how to put it back together, so she found another clock to try. But again, as soon as she opened the back, everything fell out. Grace opened five more alarm clocks. When Grace's parents discovered the clocks, they did not scold her. In fact, her mother let her have one alarm clock just so she could experiment on it.
In most sources (even the Smithsonian!), Grace is credited as using the term "debug" for the first time in a journal entry about finding a moth in the Mark I. However, research shows that she was not actually the one to first use this term. Read this article for more information:
World's FIrst Computer Bug
Grace had a clock on her wall that ran backwards. “Humans are allergic to change,” she said. “They love to say, ‘We’ve always done it this way.’ I try to fight that. That’s why I have a clock on my wall that runs counter-clockwise.”
In her office at the Pentagon, Grace flew a pirate flag.
In 1969, the Data Processing Management Association awarded Grace Hopper the first ever computer science Man of the Year Award. (Not the first WOMAN, but the first PERSON!)
When Grace Hopper retired, she was the oldest active duty officer in the United States armed forces, at 79 years old.
