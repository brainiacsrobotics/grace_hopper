The Life of Grace Hopper

December 9, 1906

Grace Brewster Murray was born in New York City. She was the first of her parents’ three children. Her father, Walter Fletcher Murray, was an insurance salesman. Her mother, Mary Campbell Van Horne, was a housewife who loved to study math.

1912 - 1923

Growing up, Grace was educated at private schools for girls: Graham School and Schoonmakers School, both in New York City, and Hartridge School in Plainfield, New Jersey.

1924 - 1928

Grace attends Vassar, an all women's college. Most women took classes in "Motherhood" or "Husbands and Wives", but Grace graduated with honors degrees in both math and physics. After Vassar, Grace enrolled at Yale University to study mathematics.

1930

Grace marries Vincent Foster Hopper, an English teacher from New York University, and becomes Grace Hopper.

1930, 1934

Grace earns her Master's degree in Mathematics from Yale University in 1930, and then her PhD in 1934. Only one woman earned a PhD in Math at Yale between 1934-1937, and that was Grace. This was her original transcript.

1931 - 1943

Throughout her graduate studies, Grace taught math at Vassar College. Many think that it was studying and teaching mathematics, which involved how to communicate meaning with symbols and words, that made Grace so adept at working on computers.

1941

Grace and Vincent separate and later officially divorce in 1945. She keeps the name Hopper.

December 7, 1941

The US is attacked,

1943 - 1946

In 1943, Grace was sworn into the US Navy to serve her country in WWII. For details about her work during the war, visit our WWII page:

1946

After WWII ends in September 1945, Grace stays on at Harvard. She would move on from the Mark I to work on the Mark II and later the Mark III.

1957-1959

Develops FLOW-MATIC for the UNIVAC 1 computer

1959

Helps to develop specifications for COBOL (Common Business-Oriented Language) based on FLOW-MATIC

1963

Awarded the Legion of Merit by Congress for her outstanding service to the military.

1966

Is forced to retire from the Navy because of her age.

1967

Is recalled to active duty by the Navy.

1967-1977

Works as Director of the Navy Programming Languages Group

1973

Grace is promoted to the rank of captain.

1977-1986

Works as Special Staff to NAVDAC, the Naval Data Automation Command.

1983

Is promoted to Rear Admiral, Lower Half (previously called Commodore).

1986

Retires a second time from the Navy, at age 79.

1986-1991

Works at Digital Equipment Corporation

January 1, 1992

Grace Murray Hopper dies and is buried at Arlington National Cemetery with full military honors.

October 21, 2016

The United States Naval Academy breaks ground on a new building for Cyber Security Studies, which will be named after Grace Hopper when completed in 2019.

November 22, 2016

Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the nation’s highest civilian honor .
